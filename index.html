About

I am a Computer Science undergraduate with a strong focus on applied Machine Learning, Computer Vision, and practical Cybersecurity. Over the past few years I’ve built end-to-end projects that solve real-world problems — from a contactless face-recognition voting system (complete with Aadhaar-based registration and duplicate-vote prevention) to a secure AES + LSB steganography tool with a user-friendly GUI. My work emphasizes robust, testable pipelines: clean data preprocessing, reproducible model training, careful evaluation (ROC-AUC, recall-focused metrics for imbalanced data), and production-aware deployment using lightweight interfaces like Streamlit and Tkinter.

Technically, I’m most comfortable with Python (Pandas, NumPy), scikit-learn, OpenCV, and NLP basics. I also use Git for version control and routinely prototype interfaces with Streamlit and Tkinter. On the security side, I’ve explored static analysis techniques for offline malware detection and studied threat modeling and defensive principles during hands-on internships.

Recent highlights include an AI internship working on ANPR and traffic-counting pipelines, and a cybersecurity internship that strengthened my understanding of network defense and incident response. I enjoy end-to-end engineering: turning a research idea into working code, validating it with real data, and packaging it so others can run it easily.

I’m actively looking for opportunities to contribute to applied AI or security-focused engineering teams where I can bring practical ML skills, strong problem-solving, and a focus on reliability. Feel free to check my projects below or reach out on GitHub or LinkedIn for collaborations or internships.
